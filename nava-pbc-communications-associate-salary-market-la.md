## Findings

### 1. The Posted Range in Context

**Nationwide picture for a literal "Communications Associate":**
The Payscale national average for Communications Associate is **$57,449** with a 10th-90th range of **$44,000 – $76,000** (n=375 profiles, updated December 2025) ([source: payscale.com](https://www.payscale.com/research/US/Job=Communications_Associate/Salary)). At Payscale's mid-career band (typically 2-5 YOE), the average is approximately **$62,500**.

By that lens, **Nava's bottom-of-band ($79,650) is already above the Payscale 90th percentile ($76,000)**, and the top ($90,000) is well above. This is **highly competitive against pure "Associate" titles**.

**Cross-checked against ZipRecruiter California (statewide):** majority of Communications Associate salaries fall up to $80,926 ([source: ziprecruiter.com California](https://www.ziprecruiter.com/Salaries/Communications-Associate-Salary--in-California)).

**The catch — title vs scope:**
The Nava role description (drafting press releases, media pitches, talking points, Q&A docs, monitoring media, supporting events, content development, CRM ownership across Salesforce/HubSpot, marketing automation in Marketo/Pardot/Mailchimp, analytics in GA/Adobe/Mixpanel) is **the workload of a Communications Specialist or Marketing Communications Specialist**, not an Associate. Comparison data points:
- Glassdoor LA Communications Specialist: **$75,595 base / $86,861 total** (median, 2025) ([source: glassdoor.com Comms Specialist LA](https://www.glassdoor.com/Salaries/los-angeles-ca-communications-specialist-salary-SRCH_IL.0,14_IM508_KO15,40.htm))
- Salary.com LA Communications Specialist: **$130,677 average; $110,714 (10th) – $152,264 (90th)** (May 2026) ([source: salary.com Comms Specialist LA](https://www.salary.com/research/salary/listing/communications-specialist-salary/los-angeles-ca))
- Glassdoor LA Marketing Communications Specialist: **~$100,600** ([source: glassdoor.com Marketing Comms LA](https://www.glassdoor.com/Salaries/los-angeles-ca-marketing-communications-specialist-salary-SRCH_IL.0,14_IC1146821_KO15,50.htm))

**Bottom line for Section 1**: At the literal "Associate" tier, $79,650-$90,000 is **strong** (above 75th percentile nationally). At the actual scope level the role describes (Specialist / Marketing Comms Specialist), the band sits at the **bottom 25th percentile** in LA. **Confidence: High** for the headline read; **Medium** for the title-vs-scope critique because aggregator definitions of "Specialist" vary.

### 7. Negotiation Levers

Tied directly to evidence in this report:

1. **Title-vs-scope realignment** (strongest lever): The job's actual responsibilities — press releases, media relations, analytics ownership, CRM/marketing-automation work across three federal portfolios — match a Specialist tier. **Ask**: "Given the scope crosses three portfolios and includes earned media work, would you consider 'Senior Communications Associate' or 'Communications Specialist' as the title, with an aligned base ceiling?" If they bump to $95K-$100K, you win without changing the title.
2. **Top of the band as the floor**: Don't accept anywhere below $90,000. Nava posts ranges as part of their stated transparent-pay commitment, so they expect candidates to land within the band. The posted band suggests they have authority to pay $90K to qualified candidates ([source: navapbc.com/careers](https://www.navapbc.com/careers)).
3. **Bonus target written in offer letter**: Nava lists "annual performance bonus" but discloses no target percentage. **Ask**: "What's the target bonus percentage for this role at-band?" If they say 5%, the all-in total $92K-$95K is fair for the title. If 10%, the offer is more attractive.
4. **Sabbatical eligibility timeline**: Stated as "after a few years of service" — pin them down. Knowing whether it's 4, 5, or 7 years materially affects the offer's long-term value. This is also a **retention indicator** — short eligibility timelines suggest Nava expects/wants long tenure.
5. **Performance review cycle and raise mechanics**: Ask for the first review cycle date, whether the band ceiling moves over time, and whether performance bonuses can extend above the listed range.
6. **Professional development budget**: Confirmed in benefits but amount unspecified. Get a number ($1K-$3K is common; $5K+ is generous).
7. **Equity / profit-sharing**: PBCs typically don't have equity, but some have profit-sharing tied to public-benefit metrics. **Ask** if any such program exists. (No public evidence of one at Nava, but worth confirming.)
8. **Severance terms in writing** (given the 2024 layoff history): Particularly relevant given confirmed 2024 RIF activity. **Ask** for written severance policy or at least a verbal commitment to industry-standard severance (typically 2-4 weeks per year of service).

**What NOT to push on**:
- Geo-adjustment for LA: Nava has explicitly stated location-agnostic pay; pushing here will not work and may signal you didn't read the careers page.
- Equity: PBC structure makes this not a viable ask.
- Signing bonus: Not standard at govtech consultancies; small chance of success.

**Confidence: Medium-High** — these levers map directly to evidenced gaps and Nava's own stated practices.
