### 1. Apple Career: The Products

Jony Ive joined Apple in September 1992 after three years at the London design agency Tangerine, where he worked on products for LG and Ideal Standard. Robert Brunner recruited him while Apple was a Tangerine client; Ive had contributed to "Project Juggernaut," an early portable computing exploration that foreshadowed the PowerBook ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jony_Ive)).

**Early period (1992-1997):** Ive's first Apple assignment was the Newton MessagePad 110's second generation. He nearly quit during this period due to Apple's lack of emphasis on design under non-Jobs management. His supervisor Jon Rubinstein persuaded him to stay by telling him Apple would "make history" once Jobs returned.

**Breakthrough (1997 onward):** Promoted to Senior VP of Industrial Design in 1997, Ive's first major project under Jobs was the iMac G3 (1998), whose translucent, candy-colored plastic case established the aesthetic template for the decade that followed.

**Full product list by category:**

Hardware:
- Newton MessagePad 110 (second generation) - 1993
- PowerBook - mid-1990s
- Power Mac G4 Cube - 2000
- iMac G3 - 1998
- iMac Pro
- Mac Pro
- MacBook line (multiple generations)
- iPod - 2001 (lead design engineer)
- iPhone (first generation) - 2007
- iPad - 2010 (design work began before iPhone shipped)
- Apple Watch - 2015 (design led from early 2012)
- AirPods (and AirPods Pro variants)

Software and interface:
- iOS 7 flat redesign - 2013 (principal designer; represented a wholesale shift away from skeuomorphism)
- Human Interface design across iOS and macOS

Architecture and environments:
- Apple Store design system
- Apple Park headquarters (Cupertino) - designed in collaboration with Norman Foster's firm; Ive's largest single project

([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jony_Ive)) ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jony_Ive#Design_work))

**Compensation and recognition:** In 2011, Ive earned a $30 million base salary plus a $25 million stock bonus. After that year Apple ceased disclosing his compensation publicly. His 2019 net worth was estimated at £192 million by the Sunday Times Rich List ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jony_Ive)).

**Relationship with Steve Jobs:** Jobs described Ive as his "spiritual partner at Apple" and said Ive had "more operational power than anyone else at Apple except me." Their offices in Cupertino were connected by a covered corridor. After Jobs died in October 2011, Ive delivered eulogies at both the Apple employee memorial and the family service ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jony_Ive#Apple_career)).

### 2. Departure from Apple (2019)

On June 27, 2019, Apple announced Ive's departure after 27 years. He had been Chief Design Officer since May 2015, one of only three C-level executives at Apple alongside CEO Tim Cook and CFO Luca Maestri. In December 2017 he resumed direct hands-on product design responsibility after a period of broader organizational oversight.

Ive's exit was gradual rather than abrupt. Apple initially remained a client of the new LoveFrom firm, a relationship that lasted until July 2022 when the consulting agreement ended.

The departure came eight years after Jobs' death. Reporting at the time noted Ive had become less engaged with day-to-day product decisions ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jony_Ive)).

### 3. LoveFrom Design Firm

**Founded:** June 2019, concurrent with Ive's Apple departure.

**Co-founders:** Jony Ive and designer Marc Newson (Newson is a longtime collaborator; Apple Watch was partly their first joint project during Newson's Apple consulting period).

**Structure:** LoveFrom operates as a design collective of roughly 60 people across disciplines including industrial design, interaction design, graphic design, UI development, architecture, CAD, filmmaking, and music production. Headquarters: Jackson Square neighborhood, San Francisco ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LoveFrom)).

**Client roster:**

- Apple (2019-July 2022): first client; consulting agreement ended after three years
- Airbnb (from October 2020): multi-year partnership for "next generation of Airbnb products and services" per CEO Brian Chesky; ongoing
- Ferrari (from September 2021): multi-year partnership; designing Ferrari's first electric vehicle, the Luce; interior revealed February 2026, exterior launch May 2026
- Moncler (partnership announced November 2021; collection launched September 2024)
- King Charles III / Sustainable Markets Initiative: designed Astra Carta project (June 2023) and Terra Carta Seal (November 2021)
- Comic Relief: Red Nose redesign, 2023
- Christie's: designed a new auctioneer's rostrum, March 2026
- Linn Products (audio equipment)
- OpenAI (became a major client via the io acquisition; LoveFrom retained independence)

([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LoveFrom))

**Brand identity:** LoveFrom unveiled its logotype (featuring a distinctive comma) in October 2021. In September 2024 it introduced an animated bear mascot named Montgomery. The firm is deliberately minimal in public-facing communication.

### 4. IO: The AI Device Company

**Founding:** 2024. Co-founded by Jony Ive, Scott Cannon, Evans Hankey (former Apple VP of Industrial Design), and Tang Tan (former Apple VP of Product Design). Headquarters: San Francisco.

**Mission:** Develop hardware products using artificial general intelligence. The founding team was described as working toward "a family of devices" ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Io_(company))).

**Backstory:** The New York Times reported in September 2024 that LoveFrom had been working with OpenAI on an AI hardware device. Ive and Sam Altman had been meeting regularly; Ive noted he asks himself "what would Steve do?" even though Jobs specifically requested he avoid that practice ([source](https://www.macrumors.com/guide/jony-ive/)).

**Funding (2024):**
- Raised $225 million from Sutter Hill Ventures, Emerson Collective, SV Angel, Maverick Ventures, and Thrive Capital
- OpenAI's Startup Fund invested in io, then acquired a 23% stake for $1.5 billion in late 2024
- Ive held 11% equity; Sam Altman held no equity

**Product vision:** The device was described as a "third core device" that could live in a pocket or on a desk. It was distinct from wearables. Initial reporting pointed to a smart speaker form factor with an integrated camera, targeting a 2027 launch ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Io_(company))), ([source](https://www.macrumors.com/guide/jony-ive/)).

### 5. Recent Developments and Acquisitions

**The acquisition:**

On May 21, 2025, OpenAI announced it would acquire io in what it called its largest acquisition to date. The deal valued io at $6.5 billion. The announcement was accompanied by a nine-minute film featuring Sam Altman and Jony Ive discussing their shared vision for "a family of devices" ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Io_(company))).

The merger completed on July 9, 2025. The 55 io employees joined OpenAI. Ive and LoveFrom did not join OpenAI; they remain an independent firm handling creative and design responsibilities for OpenAI as a client. The three other co-founders (Cannon, Hankey, Tan) joined OpenAI's team directly.

**Post-acquisition developments (2026):**

- The hardware release was delayed from 2026 to early 2027 ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Io_(company))).
- Wired reported that OpenAI abandoned the "io" branding due to trademark litigation. A new product name has not been publicly announced as of April 2026 ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Io_(company))).
- LoveFrom's Ferrari Luce EV work is progressing: interior design revealed February 2026, exterior to launch May 2026 ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LoveFrom)).
- Ive was appointed as a trustee of the British Museum in June 2025 ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jony_Ive)).

**Honors and institutional roles:**
- Appointed a Knight Commander of the British Empire (KBE) in 2012; formally entitled "Sir Jony Ive"
- Chancellor of the Royal College of Art (2017-2022)
- British Museum trustee (appointed June 2025)
