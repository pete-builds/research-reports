## Findings

### Current Roles and Titles

Ayham Boucher holds multiple overlapping roles at Cornell University [source: https://bowers.cornell.edu/people/ayham-boucher]:

- **Head of AI Innovations** for Cornell Information Technologies (CIT) at the Ithaca campus
- **Head of AI Innovations** for Information Technologies & Services (ITS) at Weill Cornell Medicine
- **AI Program Director** and **Director of the AI Innovation Lab** [source: https://qatar-weill.cornell.edu/event/2025/metc/speakers/profile/ayham-boucher]
- **Lecturer / Visiting Academic in Information Science** at Cornell Bowers College [source: https://bowers.cornell.edu/people/ayham-boucher]
- **Co-chair of the AI Innovation Accelerator**, a collaborative network of over 80 member organizations across higher education [source: https://qatar-weill.cornell.edu/event/2025/metc/speakers/profile/ayham-boucher]

His office is in Gates 213, Ithaca. Contact email: ab2325@cornell.edu [source: https://bowers.cornell.edu/people/ayham-boucher].

### Education

M.Sc. in Computer Engineering from Syracuse University [source: https://bowers.cornell.edu/people/ayham-boucher].

### Career Timeline at Cornell

- **2016**: Began working on AI projects within the College of Agriculture and Life Sciences (CALS) and the Legal Information Institute (LII). Early work included helping farmers optimize usage of 50 million acres of land and making legal information freely accessible online [source: https://hr.cornell.edu/stories/staff-stories-ayham-boucher].
- **2021**: Cornell launched the Cornell AI Initiative, creating a path for broader AI work [source: https://hr.cornell.edu/stories/staff-stories-ayham-boucher].
- **September 2024**: The AI Innovation Lab was officially launched under Boucher's leadership [source: https://hr.cornell.edu/stories/staff-stories-ayham-boucher].
- **January 2025**: Organized the inaugural AI Innovation Lab Demo Day. First cohort projects included streamlining information delivery for academic advisors, automating Cornell's travel reimbursement approval process, and enhancing website accessibility compliance through AI-driven systems [source: https://hr.cornell.edu/stories/staff-stories-ayham-boucher].

### AI Innovation Lab

The Lab currently manages approximately 24 active projects [source: https://armorysv.substack.com/p/how-ai-is-transforming-higher-ed]. Notable projects include:

- **Clinical Education**: Partnered with the veterinary school and Qatar medical campus to analyze feedback patterns and strengthen communication during student rotations [source: https://armorysv.substack.com/p/how-ai-is-transforming-higher-ed]
- **Academic Advising**: Created tools providing advisors with comprehensive student profiles, consolidating data from multiple systems [source: https://armorysv.substack.com/p/how-ai-is-transforming-higher-ed]
- **Administrative Functions**: Streamlined invoice processing, purchase orders, and research compliance [source: https://armorysv.substack.com/p/how-ai-is-transforming-higher-ed]
- **Cross-disciplinary Research**: Paired non-coding researchers in humanities and law with technical expertise to leverage LLMs [source: https://armorysv.substack.com/p/how-ai-is-transforming-higher-ed]

The Lab operates through two-week AI bootcamps for students and project sprints emphasizing rapid iteration [source: https://armorysv.substack.com/p/how-ai-is-transforming-higher-ed].

### Teaching (eCornell and On-Campus)

Boucher is the instructor for at least three eCornell courses, all 2-week online formats with 8-10 hours/week:

1. **Building AI Agents with Tools, Memory, and Agentic Architecture** - covers agentic frameworks, prompt chaining, routing, parallelization, reflection loops, agent communication protocols, handoffs, and Model Context Protocol (MCP) [source: https://ecornell.cornell.edu/courses/artificial-intelligence/building-ai-agents-with-tools-memory-and-agentic-architecture/]
2. **Context Engineering with Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)** - covers RAG fundamentals, chatbot building, Text-to-SQL, performance tuning, and GraphRAG [source: https://ecornell.cornell.edu/courses/artificial-intelligence/context-engineering-with-retrieval-augmented-generation-rag/]
3. **Creating Strategic Value with AI** - covers AI strategy, governance, ethics, organizational maturity, data infrastructure, change management, and risk mitigation [source: https://ecornell.cornell.edu/courses/artificial-intelligence/creating-strategic-value-with-ai/]

On campus, he teaches **INFO 5940 - Special Topics in Information Science** (3 credits), taught Fall 2024 and Spring 2025, with Fall 2026 listed [source: https://www.coursicle.com/cornell/professors/Ayham+Boucher/].

### Technical Expertise

His core expertise is in building LLM-powered applications, with particular depth in [source: https://bowers.cornell.edu/people/ayham-boucher]:
- Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems
- AI Agents
- Data Science
- Natural Language Processing
- Human-Computer Interaction and Design

In August 2024, he presented on RAG and introduced a custom text splitter to enhance RAG application performance at the Internet2 Community AI Initiatives meeting [source: https://spaces.at.internet2.edu/display/CAI/2024-08-01+-+RAG+with+Ayham+Boucher].

### Ethical Framework

Cornell under Boucher's leadership rates all AI projects on ethical feasibility, not just technical viability. Admissions decisions represent a "hard no" for AI, while administrative automation represents appropriate deployment. He emphasizes selective data curation in RAG systems for trustworthiness [source: https://armorysv.substack.com/p/how-ai-is-transforming-higher-ed].
