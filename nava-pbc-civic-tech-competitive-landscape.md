## TL;DR

Nava PBC competes for state SNAP, UI, PFL, and Medicaid work against three vendor groups: Deloitte and the Big Four consultancies (the dominant incumbents in state Medicaid eligibility and pandemic-era UI builds), state-benefits operators like MAXIMUS / Conduent / Gainwell / Accenture, and a small set of civic-tech-native peers (Ad Hoc, Truss, Coforma, Skylight, Agile Six, Fearless). Nava has documented active state contracts in Pennsylvania (SNAP error-rate reduction, awarded December 2025), Illinois (UI claimant portal, March 2026), Maryland (FAMLI paid family leave, October 2024 + a 7-year digital services contract), Minnesota (paid leave, March 2025), Massachusetts (PFML and child care), Vermont (integrated eligibility), New Jersey (UI claimant portal), California (UI and crisis assistance), Alaska (benefit administration), and New York (student aid, not in the 9-state list but signals NY presence) ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/news)) ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/case-studies)). The strongest state-buyer messaging anchors to documented incumbent failures: Deloitte's uFACTS unemployment systems suffered up to $22.2 billion in fraud across 8 states ([source](https://www.forbes.com/sites/thomasbrewster/2022/10/31/covid-pandemic-fraud-hits-billions-despite-deloitte-contracts-worth-hundreds-of-millions/)); Deloitte holds Medicaid eligibility contracts in 25 states covering 53 million enrollees with "ongoing and nationwide" errors flagged in an FTC complaint ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)); and Conduent (the Xerox spinoff that runs payment processing and EBT for many states) disclosed a breach affecting 26+ million people in 2024-2026, including Medicaid and benefit data, with Texas alone reporting 15.4 million impacted ([source](https://cybersecuritynews.com/conduent-data-breach/)) ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conduent)). Nava's defensible counter-positions are PBC charter governance, open-source no-lock-in delivery (Strata + the February 2026 Beam acquisition), and a documented multi-state PFL track record (Massachusetts, Maryland, Minnesota).

## 1. State Program Landscape

This section maps the four program domains Nava sells into. Each has different incumbents, different funding pipes, and different failure patterns. A state HHS commissioner or labor commissioner is buying within one of these four buckets, not "civic tech" as an abstraction.

### 1a. SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program)

**The federal-state structure.** SNAP is federally funded (USDA Food and Nutrition Service) but state-administered. Each state runs eligibility, enrollment, and payment through its own IT systems (often shared with Medicaid in an "integrated eligibility" system). USDA FNS oversees error rates and can sanction states for high payment error rates.

**Current pressure points (relevant for selling).** SNAP payment error rates spiked during and after the pandemic; FNS has been pushing states to reduce errors. Federal H.R. 1 (2025 reform act) added Medicaid community engagement requirements that touch SNAP work-requirement adjacency. Application interfaces are often the legacy IBM/CGI/Deloitte stack with poor mobile usability.

**Known vendors at the SNAP layer.**
- **Deloitte** embedded in state integrated eligibility systems (Texas TIERS, Florida ACCESS, Georgia, Tennessee, Kentucky, Arkansas, Colorado, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island) ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)).
- **Conduent** for SNAP EBT card issuance, payment processing, and back-office services across many states ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conduent)).
- **MAXIMUS** for state SNAP call centers and eligibility administration ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maximus_Inc.)).
- **Accenture, Tyler Technologies, CGI** for state IES and case management.

**Where Nava is in play.** Nava won a contract on December 23, 2025 with Pennsylvania to "reduce SNAP payment error rate through improved eligibility, processing, and support services with modern, open source technology" ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/news)). Nava also pursues SNAP work via Minnesota's MNbenefits and Vermont's integrated eligibility footprint. Nava Labs received a grant from the Center for Civic Futures to "pilot AI tools with states to help modernize and automate community engagement verification" for SNAP and Medicaid ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/news)).

### 1b. Unemployment Insurance (UI)

**The federal-state structure.** UI is administered by state labor agencies. Federal DOL provides funding (including the multi-year UI Modernization grants funded under ARPA) and sets baseline requirements. State UI IT systems are notoriously legacy (COBOL mainframes were the headline of 2020 COVID coverage).

**Current pressure points.** COVID exposed catastrophic UI IT brittleness. States are still spending federal modernization dollars to fix it. Fraud detection is a top state buyer concern, and the documented fraud across Deloitte uFACTS deployments is the single most important data point for any Nava UI sales conversation.

**Known vendors at the UI layer.**
- **Deloitte uFACTS** deployed in California, Colorado, Illinois, New Mexico, New York, Ohio, Virginia, and Wisconsin during COVID under contracts worth at least $410M combined; documented fraud up to $22.2B in those states, up to $3.2B specifically through uFACTS deployments ([source](https://www.forbes.com/sites/thomasbrewster/2022/10/31/covid-pandemic-fraud-hits-billions-despite-deloitte-contracts-worth-hundreds-of-millions/)). Ohio: contract ballooned from $10M to $122M while $166M in fraud was paid through the Deloitte system in FY2020-21 alone ([source](https://www.forbes.com/sites/thomasbrewster/2022/10/31/covid-pandemic-fraud-hits-billions-despite-deloitte-contracts-worth-hundreds-of-millions/)). Florida's CONNECT system (also Deloitte-built) was a high-profile press disaster ([source](https://www.cbsnews.com/miami/news/deloitte-floridas-overwhelmed-connect-jobless-system/)) ([source](https://www.miamiherald.com/news/politics-government/state-politics/article241651276.html)).
- **Sagitec, Geographic Solutions, FAST Enterprises** are other legacy UI tax-and-benefit platform vendors. (Mentioned by category; not deeply researched in this update.)
- **Civic-tech-native firms** including Nava, Truss, and others are now in the modernization conversation post-2020.

**Where Nava is in play.** Nava won a contract on March 2, 2026 with the Illinois Department of Employment Security to "modernize their unemployment insurance claimant portal and application" ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/news)). Nava had previously done UI work with New Jersey (claimant portal revamp) and California (two cloud-based UI services built in three months during COVID) ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/case-studies)). Nava also has a federal DOL contract conducting "user research providing human-centered modernization recommendations" and outlining state UI modernization using federal funding ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/case-studies)).

### 1c. Paid Family Leave (PFL / PFML)

**The state structure.** PFL is a state-by-state benefit, with no federal program. Programs in some form exist in CA, NY, NJ, MA, RI, WA, OR, CT, CO, DC, DE, MD, MN, VT, ME. Several launched within the past 5 years. Because PFL is new in most states, there are fewer entrenched incumbents than in Medicaid or SNAP, which makes it the most winnable Nava lane.

**Current pressure points.** PFL programs often have aggressive launch deadlines tied to legislation. They need claim intake, employer reporting, eligibility, payment, and integration with disability and other state systems. Build-vs-buy patterns vary: California uses EDD infrastructure; newer states are building purpose-built systems.

**Known vendors at the PFL layer.**
- **FINEOS** is the Irish-listed claims-management vendor heavily used by state PFL programs (mentioned by category, not deeply researched in this update).
- **Nava** has documented prime positions on Massachusetts, Maryland, and Minnesota PFL.

**Where Nava is in play.** Nava itself claims to have "won prime positions on three of 14 PFML implementations nationwide" ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/case-studies)). Specifically documented:
- **Massachusetts PFML** with multiple workstreams: user research, agile adaptation to legislation changes, intermittent leave reporting, API layer connecting disparate systems, application in five languages, pilot launch approach. Per the Nava "Our story" page, the MA PFML system processed over $600M in claims for approximately 90,000 claimants ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/story)) ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/case-studies)).
- **Maryland FAMLI** with a prime contract awarded October 24, 2024 "to build and launch a new paid family and medical leave program that's human-centered, flexible, and future-proof" ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/news)).
- **Minnesota Paid Leave** with a contract awarded March 12, 2025 "to deliver a program that's human-centered, adaptable, and resilient" ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/news)).

The Nava 2025 Public Benefit Report positions Nava as "one of the country's premier paid family and medical leave vendors" ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/news/effective-government-services)).

### 1d. Medicaid

**The federal-state structure.** State-administered, federally co-funded (FMAP). Two big IT layers: Eligibility & Enrollment (E&E) systems determine who qualifies; Medicaid Management Information Systems (MMIS) handle claims, providers, and payments. CMS publishes the Medicaid Information Technology Architecture (MITA) framework and CMS Certification standards. Federal CMS pays 90% of E&E and MMIS development costs, which is why these contracts are huge.

**Current pressure points.** The post-pandemic "unwinding" of continuous Medicaid enrollment exposed massive system errors. KFF data: ~22.8 million people had been removed from Medicaid as of June 4, 2024; nearly 1 in 4 disenrolled adults became uninsured, and nearly half were able to re-enroll, suggesting many shouldn't have been dropped ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)). State auditors are also under pressure on the IES side.

**Known vendors at the Medicaid layer.**
- **Deloitte (E&E)** has 25 states, ~53 million enrollees, contracts worth at least $5 billion combined. Documented errors in Tennessee, Texas, Florida, Georgia (where the state's own assessment said the eligibility system "lacks flexibility and adaptability"), Kentucky (25,000+ incorrect termination notices, 50,000-case backlog), Rhode Island (29,000 delayed Social Security payments during 2016 launch), Colorado (67% of sampled notices had errors in 2020; 90% by 2023), Arkansas, Pennsylvania ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)).
- **Gainwell Technologies (MMIS)** is the DXC Technology spinoff. Operates as Medicaid fiscal agent for multiple states (West Virginia, Ohio Single Pharmacy Benefit Manager, others) ([source](https://bms.wv.gov/providers/gainwell-technology)) ([source](https://spbm.medicaid.ohio.gov/)).
- **MAXIMUS** runs state Medicaid call centers, eligibility administration, Medicare 1-800 line, $5.3B FY24 revenue, 39,600 employees ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maximus_Inc.)).
- **Conduent** handles Medicaid screening, healthcare billing, prepaid card processing ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conduent)). Massive breach disclosed in 2025-2026 (see Section 4).
- **Accenture, CGI, IBM** are also active.

**Where Nava is in play.** Nava launched a "nationwide effort to build an open-source software solution that supports states in meeting" the H.R. 1 Medicaid community engagement requirements (August 14, 2025) ([source](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/nava-launches-nationwide-effort-to-build-open-source-solution-for-implementing-hr-1-302530490.html)). Vermont integrated eligibility (Medicaid + SNAP + cash assistance) is documented Nava work ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/case-studies)). Nava Labs received a Center for Civic Futures grant to pilot AI tools for SNAP and Medicaid community engagement verification ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/news)).

## 3. The Competitor Set (State-Program Lens)

### 3a. Deloitte (the dominant target)

Deloitte is the most important competitor for a state-portfolio marketing specialist to be ready to talk about. Two product lines matter:

**Deloitte uFACTS (Unemployment Insurance Framework for Automated Claims and Tax Services).** Deployed during COVID in California, Colorado, Illinois, New Mexico, New York, Ohio, Virginia, and Wisconsin under contracts worth at least $410 million combined. Documented fraud across those states: up to $22.2 billion; uFACTS deployments alone: up to $3.2 billion ([source](https://www.forbes.com/sites/thomasbrewster/2022/10/31/covid-pandemic-fraud-hits-billions-despite-deloitte-contracts-worth-hundreds-of-millions/)). Ohio contract grew from $10M to $122M while $166M in fraud was paid through the Deloitte system in a single fiscal year. Florida's CONNECT system (also Deloitte-built) failed under COVID load and triggered a class-action and ongoing scrutiny ([source](https://www.cbsnews.com/miami/news/deloitte-floridas-overwhelmed-connect-jobless-system/)) ([source](https://www.miamiherald.com/news/politics-government/state-politics/article241651276.html)). A 2020 data breach exposed up to 240,000 unemployment insurance applicants' personal information in Colorado, Illinois, and Ohio; Deloitte settled for $4.95 million ([source](https://www.forbes.com/sites/thomasbrewster/2022/10/31/covid-pandemic-fraud-hits-billions-despite-deloitte-contracts-worth-hundreds-of-millions/)).

**Deloitte Medicaid eligibility systems.** Holds contracts in 25 states with ~53 million Medicaid enrollees; contracts worth at least $5 billion combined; the National Health Law Program filed an FTC complaint alleging "ongoing and nationwide" errors and "unfair and deceptive trade practices" ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)). Specific documented state problems: Tennessee (DiJuana Davis case, autofilled wrong address, two-month coverage loss); Kentucky (25,000+ incorrect termination notices, 50,000-case backlog); Rhode Island (29,000 delayed SSI payments at September 2016 launch); Colorado (67% error rate in 2020; 90% by 2023); Arkansas (weeks-long data fix delays); Georgia (state's own 2023 assessment said the eligibility system "lacks flexibility and adaptability") ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)).

**Deloitte's standard public defense.** "Medicaid eligibility technology is state-owned. Agencies direct their operation. Decisions about policies and processes are theirs." Spokesman Kenneth Smith called the FTC complaint "without merit" ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)). Marketing should NOT claim Deloitte "ran" the systems in a way that hands them this denial; frame the comparison as "Nava designs for state ownership; Deloitte designs for vendor dependency."

## 4. Where Nava Credibly Wins (State Lens)

**1. Public benefit corporation governance maps to state HHS values.** State HHS leaders typically have a public-service ethos. A PBC charter that legally binds Nava to mission is a tangible answer to "will you still care about this contract in year four?" Deloitte, Accenture, MAXIMUS, Conduent, and Gainwell are conventional for-profits with shareholder-return fiduciary duty ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/insights/why-nava-public-benefit-corporation)). High confidence.

**2. Open source + no-lock-in = state CIO peace of mind.** State CIOs have repeatedly been burned by proprietary IT stacks. Nava's Strata suite (now with Beam's benefits-payment capability after the February 2026 acquisition) is open-source and avoids vendor lock-in ([source](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/nava-acquires-beam-to-accelerate-modern-open-benefits-delivery-302677811.html)). Beam has already disbursed over $400 million as a payment processor for 300,000+ recipients across 39 states. KFF Health News on Deloitte: "states become very dependent on the consultant for operating complex systems of all kinds" ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)). This is a direct contrast Nava can make.

**3. Documented multi-state PFL portfolio.** Nava is the closest thing to a category-defining PFL vendor: Massachusetts (live, $600M+ processed), Maryland (prime contract for FAMLI launch), Minnesota (paid leave build). Three states is a track record nobody else in the civic-tech-native cluster matches.

**4. Documented Deloitte-incumbent attack surface.** Every state Nava sells into has Deloitte-adjacent failure stories in the public record. Marketing should pre-package state-specific Deloitte talking points (e.g., "in Colorado, 67% of sampled Medicaid eligibility notices had errors in 2020; that rose to 90% by 2023") and route them by buyer geography ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)).

**5. The 2025 federal in-house collapse means states need vendors more than ever.** With 18F shut down in March 2025 ([source](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/reorganization/2025/03/after-rocky-history-gsa-shuts-down-18f-office/)) and USDS reorganized into DOGE Service in January 2025 ([source](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/establishing-and-implementing-the-presidents-department-of-government-efficiency/)), states that previously got free federal technical assistance from 18F's state-and-local arm now have to buy that capability. Nava is the natural successor: same lineage, same methods, available on contract.

## 5. Where Competitors Still Win

**1. Scale.** Deloitte ($65B global revenue ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors))) and MAXIMUS (39,600 employees, $5.3B revenue ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maximus_Inc.))) and Conduent (54,000 employees ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conduent))) operate state-by-state at a throughput Nava cannot match. For statewide operate-and-run Medicaid eligibility contracts in big states, the incumbents still win.

**2. Incumbency.** Deloitte has 25 Medicaid eligibility states locked in. Re-bidding those is slow, political, and risk-averse. Nava wins on margins (PFL, modernization phases, AI-augmented work, error-rate reduction subcontracts) but won't displace Deloitte's installed base in a single procurement cycle.

**3. Call-center and back-office throughput.** MAXIMUS and Conduent run thousands of seats. Nava does not. When a state needs millions of calls answered per month, that's not Nava's lane.

**4. Cybersecurity and infrastructure pure-plays.** Booz Allen, IBM, Accenture have larger state cybersecurity practices. Nava's positioning is service delivery, not cyber.

## 6. Marketing Implications (State Buyers)

For each angle: claim, supporting evidence, target buyer (state HHS commissioner, state Medicaid director, state Labor commissioner, state CIO), watch-out.

### Angle 1: "Your Deloitte UI system lost states billions during COVID. The incumbent has already failed in this exact program category."

- **Claim.** In states where Deloitte deployed pandemic UI tech, fraud reached billions; the incumbent's record in your exact buyer's program is publicly documented.
- **Evidence.** $410M+ in Deloitte UI contracts across 8 states (CA, CO, IL, NM, NY, OH, VA, WI); up to $22.2B in fraud in those states; up to $3.2B specifically through uFACTS; Ohio contract from $10M to $122M; California's EDD estimate of total fraud at $20B by Oct 2022 ([source](https://www.forbes.com/sites/thomasbrewster/2022/10/31/covid-pandemic-fraud-hits-billions-despite-deloitte-contracts-worth-hundreds-of-millions/)).
- **Target buyer.** State Labor commissioners and state UI directors, especially CA, CO, IL, NM, NY, OH, VA, WI. Nava already has IL UI work and NJ UI work; these are warm references.
- **Watch-out.** Deloitte will say the state owned the system. Don't frame as "Deloitte ran fraud"; frame as "Deloitte's design choices, deployed at speed, failed at scale, and the bill came to state taxpayers."

### Angle 2: "Deloitte's Medicaid eligibility errors are now a documented FTC complaint. We design for state ownership, not vendor dependency."

- **Claim.** Deloitte's state Medicaid eligibility systems have generated documented, persistent errors across 25 states; an FTC complaint is on file. Nava's open-source approach makes the state, not the vendor, the system owner.
- **Evidence.** 25 states, 53M enrollees, $5B+ contracts; FTC complaint alleging "ongoing and nationwide" errors; Tennessee, Kentucky, Rhode Island, Colorado, Arkansas, Georgia specific incidents documented; nearly 1 in 4 disenrolled adults became uninsured during unwinding ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)). Counter-position: Strata + Beam, open-source, $400M+ already disbursed across 39 states ([source](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/nava-acquires-beam-to-accelerate-modern-open-benefits-delivery-302677811.html)).
- **Target buyer.** State Medicaid directors and state HHS commissioners with active Deloitte contracts, especially in re-bid years.
- **Watch-out.** Don't promise to displace Deloitte wholesale; frame as "we can take the modernization piece while you keep what works."

### Angle 3: "Your back-office vendor was just in one of the largest US data breaches of 2025-2026. We're not."

- **Claim.** Conduent, the Xerox spinoff that runs SNAP EBT, Medicaid screening, and welfare prepaid cards across many states, disclosed a breach affecting 26+ million people, with Texas alone reporting 15.4M impacted.
- **Evidence.** Conduent breach: unauthorized access October 21, 2024 to January 13, 2025; 8TB of data including SSNs and medical histories exfiltrated by Safepay ransomware group; Texas 15.4M, Oregon 10.5M ([source](https://cybersecuritynews.com/conduent-data-breach/)) ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conduent)) ([source](https://www.inc.com/chris-morris/conduent-breach-how-to-know-if-youre-affected-largest-data-breaches-u-s-history/91308076)). CEO Cliff Skelton replaced January 2026 ([source](https://www.crn.com/news/channel-news/2026/conduent-switches-out-ceos-three-months-after-restructuring)).
- **Target buyer.** State CIOs, state CTOs, state CISOs, state HHS commissioners whose programs flow through Conduent. Worth a targeted FOIA / public-records search to identify Conduent-dependent state programs.
- **Watch-out.** Sensitive area. Lead with "we want your beneficiary data to be safe and your modernization roadmap to reduce single-vendor risk," not "Conduent is bad." Tone matters.

### Angle 4: "The PBC charter is corporate law, not a tagline. State HHS leadership keeps the same vendor for a decade if it works; PBC governance is your insurance that we'll still care."

- **Claim.** Nava's mission is written into its corporate charter as a fiduciary duty. Shareholders can vote out the board if Nava strays. Deloitte / MAXIMUS / Conduent / Gainwell are conventional for-profits with no such constraint.
- **Evidence.** Nava's own explanation: "If, somehow, Nava were to ever stray from our focus on improving government services, Nava's shareholders" can act on it ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/insights/why-nava-public-benefit-corporation)). - **Watch-out.** Procurement officers can be skeptical of social-enterprise framing. Lead with the corporate-law mechanics, not warm language.

### Angle 5: "We've launched paid family leave in three states. Nobody else in the modern stack has."

- **Claim.** Nava is the only civic-tech-native vendor with documented multi-state PFL launches.
- **Evidence.** Massachusetts ($600M+ processed, 90,000+ claimants, 5-language application) ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/story)) ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/case-studies)); Maryland FAMLI prime contract awarded October 2024 ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/news)); Minnesota Paid Leave contract awarded March 2025 ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/news)). ### Angle 6: "States lost their free federal technical assistance in 2025. We're the lineage that filled the gap."

- **Claim.** 18F's state and local team and USDS's state work used to give states free or low-cost modern delivery support. Both are gone. Nava's founders came out of the HealthCare.gov rescue lineage that birthed those teams; the work continues at Nava.

## 8. Federal and Broader Civic Tech Context (Background)

This section was the lead in the original version of this report; in the state-portfolio re-aim it's background context for understanding why state buyers behave the way they do.

**The procurement-reform legacy.** A decade of work by 18F and USDS produced the modular contracting playbook, the Digital Services Playbook, the TechFAR Handbook, and de-risking guidance ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Digital_Service)) ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/18F)). State digital service offices in California, Colorado, Massachusetts, and elsewhere adopted these methods. That's why a Nava-style modular procurement pitch lands with sophisticated state CIOs but can fall flat with older procurement officers.

**The 2025 federal reset.** 18F was eliminated March 1, 2025 ([source](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/reorganization/2025/03/after-rocky-history-gsa-shuts-down-18f-office/)). USDS was renamed to DOGE Service January 20, 2025; dozens dismissed in February 2025, 21 remaining resigned collectively February 25, 2025 ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Digital_Service)). IRS Direct File ended for Filing Season 2026 ([source](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/it-modernization/2025/11/irs-direct-file-will-not-be-available-in-2026-agency-tells-states/)). DOGE's "temporary organization" inside USDS is scheduled to dissolve July 4, 2026 ([source](https://www.nextgov.com/digital-government/2025/11/doge-no-longer-has-centralized-leadership-under-white-house-tech-team-personnel-head-says/409765/)). For state buyers, the take-away is: the free federal technical assistance pipeline is closed.

**Nava's federal portfolio (context, not lead).** Nava holds prime contracts at OPM (Customer Experience Modernization) ([source](https://fedscoop.com/opm-nava-corp-it-modernization/)), CMS (Blue Button 2.0 / BFD $22.6M ([source](https://orangeslices.ai/nava-wins-23m-cms-blue-button-2-0-bb2-0-beneficiary-fhir-data-server-bfd-contract/)), MyMedicare.gov ([source](https://blog.navapbc.com/nava-pbc-awarded-contract-to-improve-mymedicare-gov-e2d82ef1afee))), IRS (Focus-Nava JV), VA (Aquia-Nava JV), HHS Grants.gov, SEC, USDA STRATUS Cloud BOA, CDC ReportStream ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/news)). For state buyers, the federal portfolio is a signal of capability, not a primary selling point.

**Why this matters for state-portfolio marketing.** State buyers care about three things: does this vendor know my program domain (Medicaid, SNAP, UI, PFL)? Does this vendor have a documented track record in a peer state? And will this vendor still care about the work in year four? Nava's PBC charter, multi-state PFL track record, and stylistic distance from Deloitte / MAXIMUS / Conduent are the three answers to those questions.
