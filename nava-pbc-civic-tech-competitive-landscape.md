## TL;DR

Nava PBC is a roughly 700-person, mission-locked digital services consultancy that grew out of the HealthCare.gov rescue and now holds prime contracts at CMS, OPM, VA (via Aquia-Nava JV), and IRS (via Focus-Nava JV), plus state benefits work in Massachusetts and others. Its CEO has flagged Deloitte as the biggest competitor [source: prompt from Nava's marketing lead], and the public record supports that: Deloitte runs Medicaid eligibility tech in 25 states under contracts worth at least $5 billion combined ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)) and was the dominant builder of pandemic-era state unemployment systems that suffered up to $22.2 billion in fraud across states where Deloitte's tech was deployed ([source](https://www.forbes.com/sites/thomasbrewster/2022/10/31/covid-pandemic-fraud-hits-billions-despite-deloitte-contracts-worth-hundreds-of-millions/)). The 2025 federal shake-up (18F eliminated in March 2025, USDS renamed to DOGE Service in January 2025, IRS Direct File shut down for Filing Season 2026) ([source](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/reorganization/2025/03/after-rocky-history-gsa-shuts-down-18f-office/)) ([source](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/establishing-and-implementing-the-presidents-department-of-government-efficiency/)) ([source](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/it-modernization/2025/11/irs-direct-file-will-not-be-available-in-2026-agency-tells-states/)) removes the in-house competition that used to displace primes like Deloitte, which both raises Nava's addressable market and changes how it must position. The strongest marketing angles lead with documented Deloitte failures, the PBC governance moat, open-source non-lock-in (especially after the Beam acquisition for Strata), and state benefits as the durable lane while federal procurement is in flux.

## 1. Market Overview

**The buy-side reality.** Federal and state agencies procure digital services through a mix of GSA Multiple Award Schedules (MAS), specialized vehicles like OASIS+, agency-specific Blanket Purchase Agreements (BPAs) such as the OPM Digital Services BPA Nava won in 2023 ([source](https://fedscoop.com/opm-nava-corp-it-modernization/)), and direct contracts at CMS, VA, and Treasury. Nava itself is registered as a self-certified small disadvantaged business and holds a GSA MAS schedule ([source](https://govtribe.com/vendors/nava-pbc-corporation-7m2l6)) ([source](https://www.gsaelibrary.gsa.gov/ElibMain/home.do/contractorInfo.do?contractNumber=47QTCA18D008M&contractorName=NAVA+PBC&executeQuery=YES)). Set-aside vehicles (Small Business, 8(a), Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned) are how most civic-tech-native firms actually win work; Truss, for example, leads with SBA 8(a) and GSA IT-70 designations ([source](https://truss.works/about-us)).

**The procurement-reform legacy.** A decade of work by 18F and USDS produced the modular contracting playbook, the Digital Services Playbook, the TechFAR Handbook, and de-risking guidance that pushed agencies away from monolithic, fixed-price, multi-year IT contracts and toward agile, user-centered, iteratively-funded delivery ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Digital_Service)) ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/18F)). This reform created the market opening that firms like Nava, Ad Hoc, Truss, and Coforma walk through.

**The Technology Modernization Fund (TMF).** A federal revolving fund that finances modernization projects across agencies; agencies pay TMF back as they realize savings. The TMF has been a multi-year vehicle for funding agile work at USDS, 18F, and the agencies they served ([source](https://fas.org/day-one-project/government-capacity/)).

**The 2025 reset.** Three events reshaped the buy side in the past 18 months and a Nava marketer should know all three by heart:

1. **USDS renamed to DOGE Service** on January 20, 2025, via executive order ([source](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/establishing-and-implementing-the-presidents-department-of-government-efficiency/)). Dozens of USDS staff were dismissed in February 2025 and 21 remaining staffers resigned collectively on February 25 ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Digital_Service)).
2. **18F eliminated** on March 1, 2025, with all roughly 90 employees locked out at midnight EST ([source](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/reorganization/2025/03/after-rocky-history-gsa-shuts-down-18f-office/)) ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/18F)).
3. **IRS Direct File ended.** The IRS told states in November 2025 that Direct File "will not be available in Filing Season 2026" ([source](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/it-modernization/2025/11/irs-direct-file-will-not-be-available-in-2026-agency-tells-states/)). Direct File had served 140,000+ accepted returns in 2024 and was expanded to 25 states in 2025, with 86% of users saying it increased their trust in the IRS ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IRS_Direct_File)).

**Why this matters for Nava marketing.** Insourced federal digital teams used to do two things: (1) compete with vendors on greenfield modernization and (2) be the agency-side champion that demanded modern delivery from the primes. With 18F gone and USDS gutted, agencies still need that capability but have to buy it. That tilts the field toward vendors who can credibly do the user-centered, modular, open-source work the insourced teams used to do. Nava and the civic-tech-native cluster are the natural successors. (**Medium confidence** on the demand-shift thesis; it is a reasonable inference from procurement structure, not a documented market-sizing study.)

## 2. Nava's Positioning

**Founding and governance.** Nava was founded in 2015 (after its founders worked on the HealthCare.gov rescue in 2013) and was structured as a Delaware public benefit corporation ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/story)) ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/insights/why-nava-public-benefit-corporation)). A PBC has a fiduciary duty to its stated public mission alongside its duty to shareholders; this is a corporate-law constraint, not a marketing label. Nava's mission is to "make government services simple, effective, and accessible to all" ([source](https://blog.navapbc.com/)). The PBC structure means shareholders can vote out the board if Nava strays from its mission ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/insights/why-nava-public-benefit-corporation)).

**Scale.** Per the February 2026 press release announcing the Beam acquisition: "Since its founding in 2015, Nava has grown from a team of six to an organization of nearly 700 employees" ([source](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/nava-acquires-beam-to-accelerate-modern-open-benefits-delivery-302677811.html)). The Yahoo Finance reprint of the 2025 Public Benefit Report announcement says "more than 600 staff members" as of October 2025 ([source](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/nava-releases-2025-public-benefit-180000772.html)). The growth to ~1,000 by EOY 2026 was provided by the parent prompt and was not independently verified in the public record reviewed.

**Signature contracts (verified).**

- **OPM Digital Services BPA (Feb 2023).** Nava selected for a multi-year contract under OPM's Chief Information Officer's five-year tech refresh plan, modernizing OPM.gov and infrastructure related to federal employee healthcare and retirement benefits ([source](https://fedscoop.com/opm-nava-corp-it-modernization/)). The G2X profile records a "prime contract with the Office of Personnel Management (OPM) for Customer Experience Modernization on October 28, 2024" ([source](https://app.g2xchange.com/companies/E9T7F9N2ERR4)).
- **CMS Blue Button 2.0 / Beneficiary FHIR Data Server (Feb 2024).** $22.6 million, 4.5-year contract awarded under GSA MAS, Small Business Set Aside, Nava prevailed over 6 other bidders ([source](https://orangeslices.ai/nava-wins-23m-cms-blue-button-2-0-bb2-0-beneficiary-fhir-data-server-bfd-contract/)).
- **CMS MyMedicare.gov (Medicare Authenticated Experience).** Three-year, $15.8M contract with CMS ([source](https://blog.navapbc.com/nava-pbc-awarded-contract-to-improve-mymedicare-gov-e2d82ef1afee)).

A separate Focus-Nava JV award for OCIO management IT support is recorded with a total contract value of $3.66M ([source](https://orangeslices.ai/nava-and-focus-jv-win-win-irs-ocio-mgt-it-support-consulting-contract/)).
- **VA AI for veteran health (via Aquia-Nava).** Nava is teamed with Aquia on a VA contract supporting AI solutions for veteran health ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/news)).
- **HR-1 implementation, multi-state (Aug 2025).** Nava announced a "nationwide effort to build an open-source software solution that supports states in meeting" the HR-1 reform requirements ([source](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/nava-launches-nationwide-effort-to-build-open-source-solution-for-implementing-hr-1-302530490.html)).

**Strategic moves in the past 18 months.**

Beam has disbursed over $400 million as a payment processor for more than 300,000 recipients across 39 states. Beam folds into Nava's Strata, "an open-source suite for modern government service delivery" ([source](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/nava-acquires-beam-to-accelerate-modern-open-benefits-delivery-302677811.html)) ([source](https://www.govconwire.com/articles/nava-beam-benefits-delivery-platform)). **Note on the CEO's "Deloitte is our biggest competitor" framing.** That framing was provided in the parent prompt and is internally credible (it matches where Deloitte and Nava compete, especially at CMS, state Medicaid, and state benefits). It was not located as a direct public quote in the sources searched. **Medium confidence** as a quotable claim externally; **high confidence** as a directional truth about overlap.

## 3. The Competitor Set

### 3a. Big Consulting

**Deloitte Government and Public Services (GPS).** Deloitte reported global revenue of $65 billion in 2023 ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)) and U.S. yearly revenues of $50-60 billion in 2021-22 ([source](https://www.forbes.com/sites/thomasbrewster/2022/10/31/covid-pandemic-fraud-hits-billions-despite-deloitte-contracts-worth-hundreds-of-millions/)). Dominant in state Medicaid eligibility tech: 25 states, ~53 million Medicaid enrollees, contracts worth at least $5 billion combined ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)). Headwinds in 2026: Deloitte is "trimming PTO, parental leave, and other benefits for some US staff" as the firm faces "challenges across both its core business lines, accounting and consulting, largely driven by AI disruption" ([source](https://www.businessinsider.com/deloitte-cuts-down-benefits-for-some-workers-big-four-ai-2026-4)). **Signature failures** (documented in detail in Section 6) include state unemployment systems during COVID and ongoing Medicaid eligibility errors.

**Accenture Federal Services.** Accenture plc is headquartered in Dublin and runs U.S. federal work through Accenture Federal Services ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accenture)) ([source](https://www.britannica.com/money/Accenture)). The public sources reviewed for this report did not surface a comparable federal-state IT failure story; that does not mean it doesn't exist, just that it didn't surface in the time-boxed search. **Open question** flagged in Section 7.

**Booz Allen Hamilton.** A McLean, VA, Fortune 500 federal contractor weighted toward defense, intelligence, and national security ([source](https://www.forbes.com/companies/booz-allen-hamilton/)). Two material 2025-2026 events:

- October 2025: Booz Allen announced "another round of layoffs after a weak quarter, citing earlier federal contract reductions and an ongoing" headwind ([source](https://virginiabusiness.com/booz-allen-hamilton-announces-new-round-of-layoffs/)).
- January 2026: Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent canceled 31 Booz Allen contracts totaling $21 million after a former Booz Allen contractor leaked tax information about wealthy taxpayers including Donald Trump. The company's stock fell more than 8% ([source](https://www.npr.org/2026/01/26/nx-s1-5689010/treasury-cancels-booz-allen-contracts-irs-leak)) ([source](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/nation/treasury-department-ends-booz-allen-hamilton-contracts-after-leak-of-trumps-tax-returns)).

**IBM Consulting and Guidehouse.** Both are part of the Big Consulting set Nava would encounter on federal civilian work. Not covered in depth here because the public sources reviewed did not produce comparable competitive-signal material. **Open question** flagged in Section 7.

### 3b. Civic-Tech-Native Firms

Signature work: rebuilt VA.gov from 500+ websites consolidated into Vets.gov within a year; Medicare Plan Finder ("40% more enrollments with zero downtime" serving 2.6 million beneficiaries); Search.gov (2,100+ federal websites, 63% increase in page views, 75% positive user satisfaction); VA Health and Benefits mobile app rated 4.8/5 with nearly 300,000 downloads ([source](https://adhoc.team/about/)). Differentiation language: "purpose-built ad hoc teams" without bureaucratic layers, AI-accelerated development, open-source, iterative research ([source](https://adhoc.team/about/)). Headcount not disclosed on the public page reviewed.

**Truss Works.** San Francisco-based; SBA 8(a) certified small business; GSA IT-70 SIN 132-51; California PQVP/ADPQ ([source](https://truss.works/about-us)). Style: embedded teams, "we don't sell solutions; we solve problems," six values including "Act Without Fear" ([source](https://truss.works/about-us)). These are smaller and rarely show up as direct competitors to Nava on large prime contracts; they're more often partners, subs, or co-bidders.

### 3c. Government Insourced Teams (post-2025 reality)

**18F.** Founded March 19, 2014, peaked at 250+ in 2018, was at 90 staff by 2024. Eliminated on March 1, 2025; staff locked out at midnight EST ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/18F)) ([source](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/reorganization/2025/03/after-rocky-history-gsa-shuts-down-18f-office/)). TTS director Thomas Shedd described the staff as "non-essential" and "non-critical" ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/18F)). Signature initiatives that outlived 18F itself: Login.gov, Cloud.gov, U.S. Web Design System, Analytics.usa.gov, and the De-risking Guide ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/18F)).

**USDS.** Founded August 11, 2014, inside OMB under Obama. By 2024 it employed 230 across 31 federal agencies and had built the Digital Services Playbook (2014), U.S. Web Design Standards (2015), TechFAR Handbook, and the COVID-19 vaccine finder (184 million visits) ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Digital_Service)). Renamed to "U.S. DOGE Service" on January 20, 2025 via Executive Order; dozens of staff dismissed in February 2025, 21 remaining resigned collectively on February 25, 2025 ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Digital_Service)) ([source](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/establishing-and-implementing-the-presidents-department-of-government-efficiency/)). As of November 2025, "DOGE no longer has 'centralized leadership' under White House" ([source](https://www.nextgov.com/digital-government/2025/11/doge-no-longer-has-centralized-leadership-under-white-house-tech-team-personnel-head-says/409765/)). The executive order set the "temporary organization" within USDS to dissolve July 4, 2026 ([source](https://www.nextgov.com/digital-government/2025/11/doge-no-longer-has-centralized-leadership-under-white-house-tech-team-personnel-head-says/409765/)).

**State digital services.** California, Colorado, Massachusetts, and others have built state-level digital service teams. These are typically Nava's customers and collaborators, not direct competitors.

**Marketing implication of the 2025 reset:** the agency-side "demanding customer" role that 18F and USDS played is now thinner. That cuts both ways: fewer in-house champions for modern delivery, but also fewer mechanisms for in-house teams to take work away from primes. For Nava's commercial positioning, the second effect probably dominates.

### 3e. Mid-Size Federal IT and Benefits Operators

**MAXIMUS.** NYSE: MMS. 39,600 employees, FY2024 revenue $5.3 billion ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maximus_Inc.)). Runs 1-800-MEDICARE, federal student loan servicing through Aidvantage (5.6 million accounts), state Medicaid administration, and veterans disability medical exams. Track record includes a 2007 $30.5M settlement over Medicaid claims falsification, NPR investigations into Social Security interception practices for foster children, 2022-23 call center strikes, and privacy breaches in Canada ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maximus_Inc.)). **MAXIMUS is the closest direct competitor to Nava in state benefits operations** (especially Medicaid eligibility administration and call centers); Nava's open-source, PBC, user-centered story is built almost in opposition to Maximus's scale-and-throughput model.

**CGI Federal.** Subsidiary of CGI Group (founded 1976; one of the largest IT consultancies globally) ([source](https://www.cgi.com/en)). Historical note: CGI Federal was the prime on the original HealthCare.gov build whose 2013 failure created the political opening for USDS/18F and, by extension, Nava itself. (Public sources reviewed in this time-boxed search confirmed CGI's existence and scale but did not surface a current narrative on its federal civilian footprint relative to Nava; **open question**.)

**Leidos, General Dynamics IT (GDIT), Peraton.** Large federal IT primes weighted heavily toward defense, intelligence, and national security. They occasionally compete with Nava on civilian agency modernization but their center of gravity is elsewhere. **Low confidence** on direct overlap in Nava's current lane; safer to treat them as adjacent rather than primary.

## 4. Where Nava Credibly Wins

**1. Public benefit corporation governance is a structural moat, not just marketing copy.** A PBC has a fiduciary duty to its public mission written into the corporate charter; if the company strays, shareholders can vote out the board ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/insights/why-nava-public-benefit-corporation)). Deloitte, Accenture, Booz Allen, Leidos, and MAXIMUS are conventional for-profits with fiduciary duties to maximize shareholder return. When an agency program manager says "I don't want a vendor that's going to disappear when the easy money runs out," PBC governance is a verifiable answer, not a slogan. (**High confidence**.)

**2. Open-source, no-lock-in delivery (especially post-Beam).** Strata is positioned as "the only market solution enabling government teams to build reliable software at SaaS speed with custom flexibility, avoiding proprietary vendor dependencies" ([source](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/nava-acquires-beam-to-accelerate-modern-open-benefits-delivery-302677811.html)). The Beam acquisition adds a payment-processing layer that has already disbursed $400M+ to 300,000+ recipients in 39 states ([source](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/nava-acquires-beam-to-accelerate-modern-open-benefits-delivery-302677811.html)). Compared to Deloitte's proprietary state-level eligibility platforms that have generated documented errors and vendor dependence ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)), "we don't lock you in" is a defensible technical and contractual claim.

**3. CMS, OPM, and IRS digital transformation as a federal track record.** Nava's prime contracts at OPM (Customer Experience Modernization), CMS (Blue Button 2.0/BFD, MyMedicare.gov, MAX), and IRS (Focus-Nava JV) are documented, multi-year, and span both technical depth (FHIR data servers) and citizen-facing UX (Medicare authenticated experience) ([source](https://fedscoop.com/opm-nava-corp-it-modernization/)) ([source](https://orangeslices.ai/nava-wins-23m-cms-blue-button-2-0-bb2-0-beneficiary-fhir-data-server-bfd-contract/)) ([source](https://blog.navapbc.com/nava-pbc-awarded-contract-to-improve-mymedicare-gov-e2d82ef1afee)) ([source](https://orangeslices.ai/nava-and-focus-jv-win-irs-digital-transformation-initiative-contract/)). This is a credible "we operate, we don't just consult" story for civilian-agency buyers.

**4. State benefits as the durable lane.** Federal procurement is in flux, but state Medicaid, SNAP, paid family leave, and unemployment systems still have to work. The 2025 Public Benefit Report positions Nava as "one of the country's premier paid family and medical leave vendors" ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/news/effective-government-services)). With Deloitte's documented Medicaid eligibility problems (Tennessee, Texas, Florida, Georgia, others) ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)) and unemployment failures during COVID ([source](https://www.forbes.com/sites/thomasbrewster/2022/10/31/covid-pandemic-fraud-hits-billions-despite-deloitte-contracts-worth-hundreds-of-millions/)), states have a credible reason to listen to an alternative.

**5. The civic-tech-native talent story.** Nava, Ad Hoc, Truss, Coforma, Skylight, Agile Six, and Fearless together represent the only meaningful concentration of designers and engineers in the federal vendor market who came up through the 18F/USDS lineage. With 18F gone and USDS gutted, this network is now the talent reservoir for that style of work. Nava is the largest member of that network at ~700 people ([source](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/nava-acquires-beam-to-accelerate-modern-open-benefits-delivery-302677811.html)) (Ad Hoc, Truss, Coforma all appear materially smaller based on available signal).

## 5. Where Competitors Still Win

**1. Scale.** Deloitte's $65 billion in 2023 global revenue ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)) dwarfs Nava's headcount and reach. For very large multi-state operate-and-run contracts (think statewide Medicaid eligibility for 5+ million enrollees), Deloitte and MAXIMUS still win on perceived scale capacity.

**2. Defense, intelligence, and classified work.** Nava is a small disadvantaged business with a CMS/OPM/IRS civilian-agency portfolio. Booz Allen, Leidos, GDIT, Peraton, and Accenture Federal Services operate at scale in TS/SCI environments. Nava does not credibly compete for classified work or DoD program-of-record IT today. (**High confidence**.)

**3. Incumbent relationships at state level.** Deloitte has 25 states locked in on Medicaid eligibility ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)). Re-bidding those is slow, political, and risk-averse. Nava can win at the margins (paid leave, modernization phases) but isn't going to displace Deloitte's installed base wholesale in a single procurement cycle.

**4. Federal IT primes' contract-vehicle position.** OASIS+, Alliant 2, T4NG, SEWP — the major federal IT vehicles favor large primes with broad past performance and small-business sub-relationships. Nava participates via small-business set-asides and JVs (Aquia-Nava, Focus-Nava) ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/news)) ([source](https://orangeslices.ai/nava-and-focus-jv-win-irs-digital-transformation-initiative-contract/)), which is the right play, but it's structurally smaller-deal-sized than what primes pursue directly.

**5. Cybersecurity and infrastructure pure-plays.** Booz Allen, IBM, and Accenture Federal Services have larger pure-play cybersecurity and cloud infrastructure practices. Nava's positioning is user-centered service delivery and modernization, not cyber as a standalone offering.

## 6. Marketing Implications

The following four messaging angles are grounded in evidence and aimed at specific buyer types.

### Angle 1: "Deloitte's state systems lost states billions. We don't build that way."

- **The claim.** State governments that bought Deloitte's anti-fraud and eligibility systems suffered documented, massive failures. Nava's open-source, user-centered approach is built to avoid those failure modes.
- **Supporting evidence.** Across California, Colorado, Illinois, New Mexico, New York, Ohio, Virginia, and Wisconsin, Deloitte deployed pandemic unemployment systems (uFACTS and related telecoms) under contracts worth at least $410 million combined. In those states, up to $22.2 billion in unemployment fraud occurred; uFACTS-specific deployments saw up to $3.2 billion in fraud ([source](https://www.forbes.com/sites/thomasbrewster/2022/10/31/covid-pandemic-fraud-hits-billions-despite-deloitte-contracts-worth-hundreds-of-millions/)). Ohio's contract ballooned from $10M to $122M while at least $166M in fraudulent applications was paid through the Deloitte system in FY 2020-21 alone (with another $1.2 billion flagged but not adjudicated). A 2020 data breach exposed personal information of up to 240,000 unemployment insurance applicants in Colorado, Illinois, and Ohio; Deloitte settled the resulting class action for $4.95 million ([source](https://www.forbes.com/sites/thomasbrewster/2022/10/31/covid-pandemic-fraud-hits-billions-despite-deloitte-contracts-worth-hundreds-of-millions/)). On Medicaid: Deloitte holds eligibility contracts in 25 states covering 53 million enrollees; the National Health Law Program has urged the FTC to investigate Deloitte for "ongoing and nationwide" errors and "unfair and deceptive trade practices" ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)). California Assemblyman David Chiu publicly said "Deloitte has continued to underperform" and called Deloitte-supported call centers a "mess" ([source](https://www.forbes.com/sites/thomasbrewster/2022/10/31/covid-pandemic-fraud-hits-billions-despite-deloitte-contracts-worth-hundreds-of-millions/)).
- **Target buyer.** State labor departments, state Medicaid agencies, state health and human services secretaries — particularly states currently under Deloitte contract whose audits have flagged issues.
- **Watch-out.** Deloitte's standard public reply is that "Medicaid eligibility technology is state-owned" and "states direct their operation" ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)). Don't claim Deloitte literally "ran" the systems in a way that gives them a clean denial. Frame Nava's pitch as "design for state ownership, not vendor dependency."

### Angle 2: "The PBC isn't a tagline. It's a corporate-law constraint."

- **The claim.** Nava's mission is written into its charter as a fiduciary duty. Conventional for-profits have a fiduciary duty to shareholder return; Nava's includes the public mission.
- **Supporting evidence.** Nava's own explanation of why it's a PBC: "If, somehow, Nava were to ever stray from our focus on improving government services, Nava's shareholders" can act on it ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/insights/why-nava-public-benefit-corporation)). The Nextgov 2021 profile independently characterized the PBC structure as a binding mission obligation ([source](https://www.nextgov.com/digital-government/2021/09/a-government-it-public-benefit-corporation-works-to-benefit-the-public/250335/)).
- **Target buyer.** Program managers and CIOs at agencies burned by vendors who lost interest after contract award. Especially compelling at CMS, VA, OPM, and state benefits agencies where multi-year operate-and-evolve work is the norm.
- **Watch-out.** Some procurement officers are skeptical of "social enterprise" labels. Lead with the corporate-law mechanics (charter, fiduciary duty, shareholder enforcement), not feel-good language.

### Angle 3: "Open by default. The Beam acquisition makes lock-in physically harder."

- **The claim.** Nava's Strata suite (now with Beam's benefits-payment capability) is open-source. Agencies that buy Strata own their stack and can switch vendors without rewriting from scratch.
- **Supporting evidence.** Strata is positioned as "the only market solution enabling government teams to build reliable software at SaaS speed with custom flexibility, avoiding proprietary vendor dependencies" ([source](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/nava-acquires-beam-to-accelerate-modern-open-benefits-delivery-302677811.html)). Beam (now part of Strata) has disbursed over $400 million as a payment processor for 300,000+ recipients across 39 states ([source](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/nava-acquires-beam-to-accelerate-modern-open-benefits-delivery-302677811.html)). CEO quote: "With Beam's technology, our Strata offering becomes broader and deeper... prevent the lock-in that only benefits vendors" ([source](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/nava-acquires-beam-to-accelerate-modern-open-benefits-delivery-302677811.html)). Contrast with the Forbes report on Deloitte: California Assemblyman Chiu's complaint, KFF Health News finding that "states become very dependent on the consultant for operating complex systems of all kinds" ([source](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2024-06-26/medicaid-for-millions-hinges-on-deloitte-run-systems-plagued-by-errors)).
- **Target buyer.** State CIOs, federal OCIO offices, agency contracting officers writing modular contract solicitations. Also resonates with policy advocates (Beeck Center, New America, TCF) who can amplify.
- **Watch-out.** Sales teams will want to talk about Strata as a product; Nava remains a services firm. Don't oversell Strata as turnkey SaaS — frame it as "open infrastructure delivered with a service wrapper."

### Angle 4: "When the in-house teams went away, the work didn't."

- **The claim.** With 18F eliminated and USDS reorganized into DOGE, agencies that depended on in-house digital service teams now have to buy that capability. Nava's lineage from the HealthCare.gov rescue makes it a natural successor.
- **Supporting evidence.** 18F shutdown March 1, 2025 ([source](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/reorganization/2025/03/after-rocky-history-gsa-shuts-down-18f-office/)) ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/18F)); USDS renamed and gutted starting January 20, 2025 ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Digital_Service)) ([source](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/establishing-and-implementing-the-presidents-department-of-government-efficiency/)). Nava's founding lineage from the HealthCare.gov rescue ([source](https://www.navapbc.com/story)).
- **Target buyer.** CIOs, chief data officers, and modernization leads at federal civilian agencies — especially those that previously partnered with 18F or USDS and have lost that capability.
- **Watch-out.** This angle is politically loaded. The 18F shutdown is a politicized event. Lead with the capability-gap story, not the politics of why the gap exists. Avoid taking sides on DOGE.

### Optional Angle 5: "Direct File proved the model. Now agencies are buying it from vendors."

- **The claim.** IRS Direct File showed that government can deliver consumer-grade user experience at scale; with Direct File canceled for FS 2026, agencies need vendors who can carry that bar.
- **Supporting evidence.** Direct File served 140,000+ accepted returns in 2024, expanded to 25 states in 2025, and 86% of users said it increased their trust in the IRS ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IRS_Direct_File)). It was canceled in November 2025 for Filing Season 2026 ([source](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/it-modernization/2025/11/irs-direct-file-will-not-be-available-in-2026-agency-tells-states/)).
- **Target buyer.** Civic-tech-friendly congressional staffers, advocacy organizations, and agency leaders quietly looking to reproduce the Direct File model for other consumer-facing services.
- **Watch-out.** Direct File's cancellation is politically charged (Intuit and H&R Block lobbied heavily for it ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IRS_Direct_File))). Don't let messaging read as a partisan grievance; frame it as a capability the country built and shouldn't lose.

## 7. Open Questions

Items the marketing team should chase down before locking copy:

1. **Nava revenue.** Not disclosed in the public 2025 Public Benefit Report or in any source reviewed. Has Nava ever filed revenue with a third-party ranking body (Inc. 5000, Washington Technology Top 100)?
2. **CEO Deloitte-rival quote on the record.** The "Deloitte is our biggest competitor" framing came from internal context. Is there a press interview, podcast, or panel where Bhobe says this publicly? If so, marketing should anchor messaging to that primary source.
3. **Nava's role in Direct File.** The Wikipedia article credits USDS and 18F as builders and notes "additional private-sector teams." Did Nava have a contracted role? If yes, was it via Focus-Nava or directly? Get a USAspending.gov or FPDS record.
4. **CGI Federal current civilian footprint.** CGI's HealthCare.gov failure is the origin myth of the civic-tech-native cluster, but their current federal civilian footprint relative to Nava wasn't surfaced. Worth a focused USAspending.gov pull.
5. **Accenture Federal Services failure narrative.** A focused search did not surface a Deloitte-comparable competitive failure story for AFS in the time available. Worth a more focused GAO and Federal News Network search.
6. **Ad Hoc, Truss, Coforma, Skylight, Agile Six headcounts.** Hard numbers would let marketing position Nava's scale against the civic-tech-native peer set more precisely.
7. **State-by-state Medicaid contract status.** Which states are mid-cycle on their Deloitte contracts and approaching re-bid? That's the actionable target list.
8. **MAXIMUS overlap.** Specifically: which states use Maximus for eligibility administration vs. Deloitte? The two run different lanes and Nava's positioning against each differs.
